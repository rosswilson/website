I was invited onto a podcast. Listen to me talk about Christmas related bugs, and my opinion on code freezes.

At the end of 2018 I was invited onto a podcast called The Guilty Tester. It's the work of David Duke, a tester that I worked with when building TV applications at the BBC.

Together with [Gem Hill](https://twitter.com/Gem_Hill) and [Matthew Bretten](https://twitter.com/matthewbretten), we discussed Christmas related software problems, with some excellent examples. I offered up my *three* favorite Christmas bugs - a very niche list, to be honest. The discussion is spread across two episodes, the first titled [When A Bug Is Born](http://theguiltytester.libsyn.com/the-guilty-tester-episode-8-when-a-bug-is-born).

The second episode is [Frozen When Your Codes Not Open](http://theguiltytester.libsyn.com/the-guilty-tester-episode-9-frozen-when-your-codes-not-open) where we discuss code freezes - especially those around the Christmas period.

Code freezes are an understandable method of avoiding production issues and downtime, though have their drawbacks. Take a listen to the episode to hear my thoughts on the subject, and do get in touch if you'd like to chat more.
